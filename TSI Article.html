Parliament passes landmark fiscal reform after marathon session

The overnight vote reshapes capital-gains treatment and the SST base for the first time in a decade — analysts call it the most consequential budget bill in years.

N
Nurul Iman
2 hours ago · 6 min read
1,240 views 3 comments Share

The chamber sat past 3am before the reform bill cleared its final reading. — TSI file photo

In a session that stretched deep into the early hours, lawmakers approved a sweeping package of fiscal measures that will reshape how investment income is taxed and how the state funds its next decade of spending. The bill passed on a narrow margin after nearly eleven hours of debate.

The reform overhauls the capital-gains framework for the first time since the early 2010s, introducing tiered bands intended to shield smaller, long-term investors while asking more of short-horizon, high-frequency activity. Officials framed the change as a rebalancing rather than a tax rise, though critics were quick to question the timing.

What changes, and when

Treasury has confirmed the new bands will take effect at the start of the next fiscal year, with a transitional window for positions already held. For most retail investors, the practical impact is expected to be modest; the heavier adjustments fall on entities and trading structures that turn over holdings rapidly.

  • Long-term holdings retain their existing, more favourable treatment.
  • A new upper band applies to short-horizon gains above a set threshold.
  • The SST base broadens gradually across selected service sectors.

“This is the most consequential budget bill we have seen in a decade — not because of what it raises, but because of what it signals about the years ahead.”

— Independent fiscal analyst, briefed on the measures

Markets took the news in stride. Early indications pointed to a muted reaction among blue-chip names, while sectors most exposed to the widened service-tax base saw sharper, if orderly, repricing. Analysts cautioned that the full effect would only become clear once the transitional guidance is published in the coming weeks.

The road ahead

Attention now turns to implementation. The central bank and revenue authority are expected to release detailed guidance shortly, and industry groups have signalled they will seek clarifications on edge cases. For readers, the immediate takeaway is simple: review your holding periods, and read the fine print when it lands.
